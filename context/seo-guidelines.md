# SEO Guidelines — Nexter WP (nexterwp.com)

General SEO rules and standards for all content published on nexterwp.com. SEO Machine must follow every section of this document for every piece of content generated — from keyword selection to publishing.

---

## 1. 🌍 Geographic & Language Focus

- **Primary markets**: Global English-speaking (US, UK, Canada, Australia)
- **Secondary market**: India (large WordPress developer base — Nexter is built by POSIMYTH)
- **Language**: English only for all blog content
- **No geo-specific URLs** — all content targets global search intent

---

## 2. 🔍 Keyword Usage Per Post

- One primary keyword per post — never try to rank for two at once
- Primary keyword appears **3–6 times** naturally — never forced
- Use LSI/semantic variants — don't repeat exact phrase unnaturally
- **First 100 words** must contain the primary keyword
- Primary keyword must appear in: H1, at least one H2, meta description, and image alt text

### Keyword Types to Target
1. **Comparison** — "Nexter vs Astra", "OceanWP alternative"
2. **Problem/fix** — "how to fix [WordPress error]", "WordPress [X] not working"
3. **Best-of lists** — "best WordPress themes for [niche]", "best Gutenberg blocks plugins"
4. **How-to** — "how to [do X] in WordPress", "how to add [feature] to WordPress"
5. **Resource** — "free WordPress [X]", "best [tools] for WordPress"
6. **Feature-specific** — "WordPress dark mode block", "WordPress countdown timer block"
7. **Free theme** — "free WordPress themes", "best free WordPress themes 2026"

### Keyword Types to Avoid
- Topics with no WordPress connection and no strong content angle
- Overly broad keywords with no buyer intent (e.g., just "website")
- YMYL topics requiring medical/legal/financial expertise
- Anything requiring misleading or clickbait content

---

## 3. 📄 Title Tag Rules

- **Format**: `[Primary Keyword]: [Benefit or Number] | Nexter WP`
- **Max length**: 60 characters
- Primary keyword must appear **near the start**
- Use numbers where possible: "7 Ways", "5 Key Differences", "10 Best"
- Add current year for competitive evergreen posts: "Best Free WordPress Themes 2026"

| ✅ Good | ❌ Bad |
|---|---|
| Best WordPress Gutenberg Blocks in 2026 \| Nexter WP | WordPress Themes and Blocks Guide |
| Astra vs Nexter: 15+ Feature Comparisons \| Nexter WP | Our WordPress Theme Comparison |
| Fix Blurry Images in WordPress (5 Methods) \| Nexter WP | How to Fix Images |

---

## 4. 📋 Meta Description Rules

- **Length**: 140–155 characters
- Must include the primary keyword naturally
- Must include a clear benefit or soft CTA
- Write for humans first — no keyword stuffing
- **Example**: "Compare Astra vs Nexter WP on speed, blocks, pricing & features. See which WordPress theme is right for your site in 2026."

---

## 5. 🔗 URL / Slug Rules

- Lowercase, hyphen-separated only
- Include primary keyword
- **5–6 words maximum** — keep it short
- No dates in URLs — content must stay evergreen
- No stop words (a, the, in, of) unless essential to readability

| ✅ Good | ❌ Bad |
|---|---|
| /blog/astra-vs-nexter/ | /blog/2026/03/astra-vs-nexter-comparison/ |
| /blog/best-gutenberg-blocks/ | /blog/the-top-10-best-wordpress-gutenberg-blocks-plugins/ |
| /blog/fix-blurry-images-wordpress/ | /blog/how-to-fix-blurry-images-in-wordpress-site/ |

---

## 6. 🏗️ Heading Structure

| Level | Use For | Rules |
|---|---|---|
| H1 | Article title only | One per page. Contains primary keyword. Matches or closely mirrors title tag. |
| H2 | Main sections | Use secondary keywords naturally. Minimum 2 per article. |
| H3 | Sub-sections inside H2 | Use long-tail or LSI keywords. Optional but recommended. |
| H4 | Tool/option names in comparisons | e.g. "#### Cloudflare Turnstile" inside a comparison H3 |

- Never skip heading levels (H1 → H3 without H2)
- Never use headings just for visual styling
- Every heading must describe exactly what follows — never vague like "More Info" or "Introduction"

---

Every post must follow this structure in order:

```
[Featured Image]

[H1 — Article Title]
[Meta info: Updated Date | Author | Reading Time]

[Intro — 2-3 short paragraphs]
[Table of Contents — for posts 1,000+ words]

[H2 — Main Section 1]
  [H3 — Sub-section if needed]
  [Content + Image]

[H2 — Main Section 2...]

[H2 — Wrapping Up]
  [2-3 sentences + soft Nexter CTA]

[Email Subscribe CTA]

[H2 — FAQs] ← Comparison & how-to posts only
  [H3 — Question?]
  [Answer: 40-80 words]

[Related Posts]
```

## 8. ✍️ Content Writing Rules

### Paragraph & Sentence Style
- Paragraphs: **2–4 sentences max** — never a wall of text
- Sentences: Mix short (under 15 words) with medium (15–25 words) — vary the rhythm
- One idea per paragraph
- Lead with the point — don't bury key information at the end
- **Active voice always**: "Nexter loads in 0.5 seconds" not "0.5 seconds is how fast Nexter loads"

### Intro Paragraph Rules
The intro must do 3 things in 2–3 short paragraphs:
1. Validate the reader's problem or confirm the topic
2. Preview what the article covers
3. Give the reader a reason to trust this article

**Never start with:**
- "In today's digital world..."
- "Welcome to our blog post about..."
- A question that reads like clickbait

### Tone
- Write like a knowledgeable colleague explaining something simply
- Direct, confident, helpful — never condescending
- Use "you" and "your" throughout
- Technical when needed — plain English first

### Do NOT Write Like This
- ❌ Corporate fluff: "Our robust, world-class solution leverages cutting-edge technology..."
- ❌ Overpromising: "This is the BEST plugin ever made!"
- ❌ Filler transitions: "Moving on to our next point..."
- ❌ Unnecessary qualifiers: "very", "really", "quite", "rather", "somewhat"

## 10. 🔗 Internal Linking Rules

- **Minimum 3, maximum 7** internal links per post
- At least 1 link → a Nexter product or feature page
- At least 1 link → the pricing page (natural anchor only)
- At least 1 link → a related blog post
- Always use `internal-links-map.md` for URLs and anchor text
- Vary anchor text — never repeat the same phrase twice in one post

### Mid-Post Related Article Links
Use italic text to reference related articles mid-post:

> *Want to [relevant goal]? Here's [how to do X / our guide on Y].*

Max 2–3 mid-article related links per post. Place at natural section breaks — never mid-paragraph.

---

## 11. 🌐 External Linking Rules

- Link to authoritative sources (WordPress.org, Google, official docs) when citing facts
- **Never link to competitor homepages**
- Competitor links allowed in comparison posts only — always use `rel="nofollow"`
- Limit to **2–3 external links** per post

---

## 12. 🖼️ Image Rules

- **Featured image**: Required on every post
- **How-to posts**: Minimum 1 screenshot per major H2 section
- **Alt text**: Required on every image — descriptive, keyword-included where natural
- **Alt text format**: `[description of what's shown] — [keyword if natural]`
- **File naming**: `nexter-blocks-pricing-table.webp` not `screenshot-1.png`
- **Format**: WebP preferred — Nexter has built-in image optimization
- **Captions**: Optional but useful for annotated screenshots

---

## 13. 📣 CTA (Call to Action) Rules

### Mid-Article Product Mention
- Mention Nexter **once or twice max** per post — never more
- Always tie it to the specific problem being solved in that section
- Link to the most specific page possible (specific block page, not just homepage)
- Keep to 1–3 sentences

### Closing CTA (in "Wrapping Up")
- 2–3 sentences max — **benefit-first, not feature-first**
- Always include a link to pricing or free download
- **Example**: "Nexter Blocks gives you 90+ Gutenberg blocks, built-in image optimization, and AI in every block — all in one plugin. [Get Nexter today](https://nexterwp.com/pricing/) and start building faster."

### Email Subscribe CTA
- Place after "Wrapping Up", before FAQs
- Heading + one-line description only — no form code needed

---

## 14. ❓ FAQ Section Rules

- **Required for**: comparison posts, how-to tutorials, error fix posts
- Minimum 4 questions, maximum 8
- Each answer: **40–80 words**
- Questions must reflect real search queries people actually type
- Use H3 for each question

**Good FAQ question formats:**
- "Is [Tool A] better than [Tool B]?"
- "Can I use [X] with WordPress?"
- "Does [Product] work with WooCommerce?"
- "Is [X] free?"

---

## 15. 🏷️ Schema & Structured Data

| Schema Type | Where to Apply |
|---|---|
| Article | All blog posts |
| Organization + WebSite | Homepage only |
| Product | Product/feature pages only (not blog posts) |

- ⚠️ **FAQPage schema** — restricted to government/healthcare sites only (Google, Aug 2023). **Do NOT use** on Nexter content.
- ⚠️ **HowTo schema** — fully deprecated, rich results removed (Google, Sep 2023). **Do NOT use.**
- ✅ **JSON-LD only** — never Microdata or RDFa.

| FAQ content | H3 question + 40–60 word paragraph answer |

## 17. 📖 Table of Contents Rules

- **Required** for all posts over 1,000 words
- Use jump links (anchor IDs on each heading)
- Place immediately after the intro paragraph, before the first H2
- Minimum 4 items, maximum 10

---

## 18. 🧠 E-E-A-T Requirements

*(Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness — applies to ALL content as of Dec 2025)*

Every post must demonstrate:
- **Experience** — Include practical examples, real use cases, or screenshots showing the tool in action
- **Expertise** — Accurate technical info. Verify facts about WordPress versions, plugin compatibility, and pricing before publishing
- **Authoritativeness** — Link to official sources (WordPress.org, plugin docs) when citing data
- **Trustworthiness** — Be fair in comparisons, acknowledge competitor strengths, never make unverifiable claims

**Practical rules:**
- Always include "Updated On: [date]" in post metadata
- Credit external sources when referencing data
- Never publish thin content under 800 words
- Include practical examples, not just theory

---

## 19. 🔄 Content Freshness Rules

- Update top-performing posts every **6–12 months**
- Always update the "Updated On" date when content is revised
- Add current year to title tags of competitive evergreen posts
- **Never delete old URLs** — redirect or update instead
- When a Nexter product feature changes, update all related posts

## 🚫 Always Avoid

- Keyword stuffing — hurts rankings and reads unnaturally
- Duplicate content — every post must be 100% unique
- Thin content under 800 words
- Generic titles with no keyword
- Clickbait that doesn't match article content
- Negative competitor commentary without factual backing
- Unverifiable superlative claims ("the #1 WordPress theme in the world")
- FAQPage or HowTo schema (restricted/deprecated by Google)
- Passive voice throughout
- Walls of text without paragraph breaks
- Images without alt text
