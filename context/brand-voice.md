# Nexter WP Brand Voice & Messaging

This document defines the Nexter WP brand voice, tone, and messaging framework. Reference this when writing all content to ensure consistency.

## Brand Voice Pillars

### 1. Confident Expert
- **What it means**: We know WordPress deeply — and we show it without showing off.
- **How it sounds**: Direct, assured, specific. We don't hedge or over-qualify. We state facts backed by benchmarks.
- **Example**: "Nexter loads in under 1 second on a default setup — no optimization tricks required."
- **Avoid**: Hype words like "world-class," "revolutionary," "robust." Vague claims without proof.

### 2. Actually Helpful
- **What it means**: We write to solve problems, not to sound impressive.
- **How it sounds**: Lead with the benefit. Skip the long intro. Get to what the reader needs immediately.
- **Example**: "Here's exactly how to set up your first block pattern in Nexter — takes about 3 minutes."
- **Avoid**: Long windup paragraphs before the point. Padding. Generic advice that applies to any tool.

### 3. Smart Colleague Energy
- **What it means**: Professional but warm. We talk like a knowledgeable colleague, not a corporate manual.
- **How it sounds**: Conversational but never sloppy. No slang. No stiffness. Just clear, human writing.
- **Example**: "If you've used theme.json before, this will feel familiar. If not — don't worry, we'll walk through it."
- **Avoid**: Corporate speak ("leverage synergies"), overly casual language ("lol," "literally"), or being condescending to beginners.

### 4. Proof-Driven
- **What it means**: Every claim earns its place with a number, benchmark, or real example.
- **How it sounds**: Specific and concrete. We replace adjectives with data wherever possible.
- **Example**: "Nexter scores 98+ on Google PageSpeed Insights — out of the box, no plugins."
- **Avoid**: "Blazing fast," "incredibly lightweight," "amazingly simple" — anything that sounds like a press release.

### 5. Empowering, Not Gatekeeping
- **What it means**: We assume our readers are smart, just at different levels. We never talk down.
- **How it sounds**: We define technical terms briefly, inline, without making readers feel behind.
- **Example**: "FSE (Full Site Editing) lets you design your entire site — header, footer, templates — from inside the WordPress editor."
- **Avoid**: Assuming everyone knows jargon. Also avoid over-explaining things to developers who clearly know their stuff.

## Tone Guidelines

### General Tone
Imagine a senior WordPress developer who genuinely wants to help — not sell. They know every edge case, can speak fluently with beginners and pros, and they skip the fluff because they respect your time. That's the Nexter voice. Professional enough to be taken seriously, approachable enough that you'd actually ask them a follow-up question.

### Tone Variations by Content Type

**How-To Guides**: Clear, step-by-step, encouraging
- "Here's what you'll do first..."
- "This part trips people up — here's why it happens and how to fix it."
- "You're done. Your theme is now configured for optimal performance."

**Strategy/Advice Content**: Thoughtful, direct, evidence-backed
- "Most themes ship with 200KB of CSS you'll never use. Nexter doesn't."
- "Faster themes aren't just a developer preference — they directly impact bounce rate and conversions."
- "Here's how to think about starter themes vs. multipurpose themes before you choose."

**Product/Feature Content**: Benefit-first, specific, no hype
- "Block patterns in Nexter give you full design control without touching code."
- "theme.json support means your design tokens live in one place — and actually work."
- "Compatible with Elementor, Bricks, Gutenberg, and all major block plugins."

**SEO/Performance Content**: Technical but accessible, data-forward
- "A 1-second delay in page load reduces conversions by 7% (Google, 2023)."
- "Nexter outputs clean, minimal HTML — no wrapper divs, no hidden scripts."
- "Core Web Vitals aren't optional anymore. Here's how Nexter helps you pass them."

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Sentence Structure
- **Vary length**: Mix short punchy sentences with longer explanatory ones. Short = impact. Long = context.
- **Active voice always**: "Nexter handles caching automatically" not "Caching is handled automatically by Nexter"
- **Average length**: 12–18 words per sentence
- **Clarity first**: If a sentence needs to be re-read, rewrite it

### Paragraph Structure
- **Length**: 2–4 sentences typically. One long paragraph is usually two short ones.
- **One idea per paragraph**: Don't stack multiple concepts. Split them.
- **White space**: Use it liberally. Dense blocks of text lose readers.

### Word Choice
- **Specific over vague**: "Loads in 0.8 seconds" not "Loads fast"
- **Active verbs**: Launch, build, configure, enable, connect, optimize
- **Cut filler words**: Remove "very," "really," "actually," "just," "simply" unless they add meaning
- **Avoid buzzwords**: Revolutionary, robust, cutting-edge, world-class, next-generation

### Terminology

**Say This** → **Not That**
- "Loads in under 1 second" → "Blazing fast" (be specific)
- "No coding needed" → "User-friendly" (too vague)
- "Full Site Editing (FSE)" → "FSE" alone on first mention (define it)
- "Get your free starter theme" → "Click here" (action-driven CTAs only)
- "Nexter does X" → "X is done by Nexter" (active voice)
- "Compatible with Elementor" → "Works seamlessly with Elementor" (confident, not hedging)

## Content Formatting

### Headlines
- **Be specific**: "How to Build a Blog with Nexter in 30 Minutes" not "Nexter Blog Tutorial"
- **Lead with benefit**: Tell the reader what they'll gain or be able to do
- **Use numbers when relevant**: "7 Reasons," "3 Steps," "Complete Guide"
- **Keep concise**: 60 characters or less for SEO-facing titles

### Subheadings
- **Make them scannable**: A reader should understand the article structure from H2s/H3s alone
- **No clever-but-vague headings**: "Step 1: Install the Theme" not "Let's Get Started!"
- **Use sentence case**: "How to configure theme.json" not "How To Configure Theme.JSON"
- **Include keywords naturally**: Don't force them, but don't avoid them either

### Lists
- **Use for steps and features**: Great for how-tos and feature comparisons
- **Keep parallel**: All items same grammatical structure
- **3–7 items ideal**: Under 3 = just write prose; over 7 = consider grouping
- **No bullet padding**: Each item should say something distinct

### Calls-to-Action
- **Action-driven**: "Get your free starter theme" not "Learn more"
- **Specific**: "Download Nexter" not "Click here"
- **Benefit-connected**: "Start building faster — download free"
- **One primary CTA per section**: Don't stack competing CTAs

## Voice Examples

### Excellent Nexter WP Voice ✅

"Most WordPress themes ship with hundreds of kilobytes of CSS, JavaScript, and template code you'll never use. Nexter takes the opposite approach. It's a lean starter theme — no predefined layouts, no forced design opinions, just a clean foundation you build on. The result: 98+ PageSpeed scores out of the box, full FSE support for developers, and a setup simple enough that non-coders can launch in an afternoon. Faster to build, faster to load, better for SEO."

**Why this works**:
- Opens with a real problem (bloated themes), not a product pitch
- Backs the performance claim with a specific number (98+)
- Addresses multiple audiences (developers + non-coders) without talking down to either
- Active voice throughout: "Nexter takes," "You build on"
- No hype words — every claim is specific

### Not Nexter WP Voice ❌

"Nexter WP is a revolutionary, world-class WordPress theme solution that leverages cutting-edge technology to deliver a robust, highly-optimized web experience for users of all skill levels. Our incredibly powerful yet user-friendly platform enables you to build stunning websites with ease, empowering your digital journey and unlocking your true potential online."

**Why this fails**:
- "Revolutionary," "world-class," "cutting-edge," "robust" — pure hype, zero proof
- "Leverages" and "empowering your digital journey" = corporate speak
- No specifics — what does it actually do? How fast? What features?
- Passive and bloated structure
- Generic enough to describe literally any product

## Audience Understanding

### Who We Write For

**Primary Audience**: WordPress site builders — developers, freelancers, and agencies building sites for clients
- Builds 1–20+ WordPress sites per year
- Cares deeply about performance and clean code
- Frustrated by bloated multipurpose themes
- Evaluates tools based on flexibility and long-term maintainability
- Active in WordPress communities (WP Tavern, WPBeginner, Twitter/X, Discord)

**Secondary Audiences**:
- **Business Owners**: Want a fast, professional site without managing developers for every change
- **General Consumers**: First-time WordPress users who want a great-looking site without coding
- **WooCommerce Store Owners**: Need speed + compatibility for their online store

### What They Care About

**Top Priorities**:
1. Site speed and Core Web Vitals scores
2. Flexibility — building without fighting the theme
3. Clean, maintainable code
4. Compatibility with their preferred builder/plugins
5. Long-term support and active development

**Pain Points**:
1. Themes that ship with too much: bloated CSS, unused features, slow load times
2. Themes that override customizations and break on updates
3. Poor FSE or block editor support in older themes
4. Locked-in designs that make every site look the same
5. Themes that conflict with popular plugins

### How to Serve Them

- **Respect their time**: Lead with the answer, then explain why
- **Be technically honest**: Developers will test our claims — only say what's true
- **Bridge skill levels**: Define technical terms for beginners without boring developers
- **Make it actionable**: Every piece of content should end with a clear next step
- **Connect to outcomes**: Don't just describe features — explain what they let you achieve

## Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] **Voice**: Sounds like Nexter WP (confident, helpful, proof-driven, human)
- [ ] **Tone**: Appropriate for the audience and content type
- [ ] **Value**: Provides genuine, actionable insights for WordPress builders
- [ ] **Clarity**: Technical concepts explained clearly — no unexplained jargon
- [ ] **Accuracy**: All performance claims, numbers, and compatibility statements are verified
- [ ] **Specificity**: Vague adjectives replaced with concrete data or examples
- [ ] **Action**: Clear next step or CTA at the end
- [ ] **Messaging**: Connects to at least one core brand message (speed, flexibility, developer-friendly)
- [ ] **Terminology**: Uses correct WordPress/web terms consistently
- [ ] **Active voice**: No passive constructions unless intentional
